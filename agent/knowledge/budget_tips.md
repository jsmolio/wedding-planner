# Wedding Budget Tips

## The 50/30/20 Guideline
A common rule of thumb allocates roughly 50% of the budget to the reception
(venue + catering), 30% to vendors and services (photography, music, flowers,
officiant), and 20% to everything else (attire, stationery, favours,
transportation, contingency).

## Prioritise What Matters Most
Sit down together and rank the elements of your wedding day by importance.
Spend more on your top three priorities and find creative savings on the rest.
Couples who prioritise early report less budget stress later.

## Build in a Contingency Fund
Set aside 5-10% of your total budget as a contingency buffer.  Unexpected costs
almost always come up — last-minute rentals, weather-related plan changes, or
rush fees.

## Track Every Expense
Use a spreadsheet or app to record every payment, deposit, and outstanding
balance.  Categorise expenses and compare estimated vs. actual spend at least
once a month.

## Negotiate and Compare
Always get at least three quotes for major vendor categories.  Many vendors are
willing to customise packages or match competitor pricing if you ask.

## Consider Off-Peak Savings
Fridays, Sundays, and winter months are generally cheaper for venues and
vendors.  An off-peak date can save 20-40% compared to a Saturday in June.

## Tipping Guide
Budget 15-20% of vendor fees for gratuities.  Tips for the coordinator, DJ,
photographer, and catering staff are customary and often overlooked during
planning.
